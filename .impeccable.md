# RentRadar — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
People actively searching for apartments to rent in Latvia (primarily Riga). Three language groups: Russian-speaking (largest), Latvian, English (expats). They're checking ss.lv multiple times a day, stressed about missing a good listing. They come to this landing page via paid/organic traffic — already motivated, low patience. The job to be done: understand the product in 10 seconds and tap the bot button.

### Brand Personality
**Fast. Reliable. Sharp.**

The bot gives a competitive edge in a tight market — this should feel like an unfair advantage, not a utility app. The emotional goal is *confident urgency*: "I'll get the apartment before anyone else." Not calm reassurance — more like a sports car dashboard. Decisive.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary references**: Apple.com, Stripe.com — generous whitespace, Geist/SF typography, precision shadows, clean hierarchy
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS templates, overly dark or neon "hacker" aesthetics, crowded UI
- **Theme**: Light mode only (no dark mode complexity)
- **Current stack**: Pure HTML/CSS/JS, Geist font (Google Fonts), no framework
- **Current palette**: `#0071e3` primary (Apple blue), `#1d1d1f` dark, `#fbfbfd` bg, `#86868b` muted
- **Current UI patterns**: Floating pill nav, soft cards (`border-radius: 32px`), bento grid, fade-up animations, dark Pro pricing card (`#0a192f`)

### Design Principles

1. **CTA above all** — Every section exists to move the user toward "Open in Telegram." Reduce friction, increase CTA visibility. The primary button should be impossible to miss.

2. **Speed is the product** — Typography should feel fast: tight letter-spacing, bold weight, confident sizing. No decorative elements that slow the eye.

3. **Apple/Stripe quality bar** — Generous padding, precise alignment, Geist font hierarchy. Shadows should be whisper-soft (`rgba(0,0,0,0.04-0.06)`). Nothing looks cheap.

4. **Trust through directness** — Copy is concrete (real prices, real intervals: "every 5 minutes"). No vague superlatives. Pricing is transparent and upfront.

5. **One emotional register: urgency** — Micro-copy, section headers, and CTAs should reinforce that good apartments disappear fast. The green pulse indicator, "Bot Online" badge, and "before anyone else" framing are correct.
